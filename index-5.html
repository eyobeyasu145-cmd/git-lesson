Chapter 1 Summary

Psychology Basics
  • Psychology: Study of behavior & mind
  • Behavior: Observable actions
  • Mental Processes: Thinking, feeling
  • Goals: Describe, explain, predict, control
  • 1879: Psychology founded
Historical Schools
  • Structuralism: Wundt & Titchener
  • Functionalism: William James
  • Behaviorism: John Watson
  • Psychoanalysis: Sigmund Freud
  • Gestalt: Whole > Sum of parts
Modern Perspectives
  • Humanistic: Free will & growth
  • Cognitive: Thinking & memory
  • Biological: Brain & genetics
  • Sociocultural: Culture & society
  • Developmental: Lifespan changes
Midterm Tips - Chapter 1

Focus on: Psychology definition (behavior + mental processes), goals of psychology, historical schools (founders and key ideas), modern perspectives, and research methods.

Sensation Basics
  • Sensation: Detection of stimuli
  • Perception: Interpretation of stimuli
  • Transduction: Energy conversion
  • Absolute Threshold: Minimum detectable stimulus
  • Difference Threshold: Smallest detectable difference
Senses
  • Vision: Eye detects light
  • Audition: Hearing sense
  • Olfaction: Sense of smell
  • Gustation: Sense of taste
  • Vestibular: Balance sense
Perception Concepts
  • Gestalt Psychology: Whole experience
  • Figure-Ground: Object vs background
  • Perceptual Constancy: Stable perception
  • Depth Perception: Judging distance
  • Illusion: Misinterpretation of stimuli
Midterm Tips - Chapter 2

Focus on: Sensation vs Perception, Thresholds (Absolute & Difference), Gestalt laws, Illusion vs Hallucination, Sensory Adaptation, and Perceptual Constancy.

Learning Basics
  • Learning: Permanent behavioral change
  • Characteristics: Change, experience, goal-directed
  • Maturation: Biological growth (not learning)
  • Law of Exercise: Practice improves performance
  • Law of Effect: Thorndike's satisfaction principle
Learning Theories
  • Classical Conditioning: Pavlov (stimulus association)
  • Operant Conditioning: Skinner (consequences)
  • Social Learning: Bandura (observation)
  • Insight Learning: Köhler (sudden understanding)
  • Cognitive Theory: Mental processes focus
Learning Concepts
  • Reinforcement: Increases behavior
  • Punishment: Decreases behavior
  • Positive Reinforcement: Adding something pleasant
  • Observational Learning: Attention, retention, reproduction, motivation
  • Trial & Error: Thorndike's repeated attempts
Midterm Tips - Chapter 3

Focus on: Learning definition, Classical vs Operant conditioning, Reinforcement vs Punishment, Social learning theory (Bandura), Insight learning (Köhler), and factors influencing learning.
